about me

Just Someone Who Likes Building Things

I'm Rohit — a BCA student at Jaipur National University who got into tech because I genuinely enjoy figuring out how things work. That curiosity has taken me into software development, web projects, and — naturally — into wanting to understand how systems can break and how to build them better.

I enjoy building things and understanding how systems work — from writing code and shipping projects to thinking about how secure systems should be designed. Security isn't my whole identity; it's more like a lens I use to think more carefully about what I build.

I've done four internships, built real tools, and I'm still very much in learning mode — which is honestly the part I enjoy most. I'd rather ship something imperfect and learn from it than wait until everything's perfect.

🛠 I Learn by Building

Projects teach me more than any course. Every build is a chance to understand something better and get sharper at solving real problems.

🔍 Security as a Differentiator

I see security as something that makes me a more thoughtful developer — not a separate identity. It shapes how I think about what I build.

📈 Always in Progress

I don't think I'll ever feel like I "know enough" — and I'm fine with that. Curiosity and consistency matter more than having all the answers.

A full-stack cybersecurity platform that scans real-world domains for exposed technologies, misconfigurations, and known CVEs — then keeps watching them. Combines technology fingerprinting, exploit intelligence, subdomain discovery, and automated alerting into a single dashboard.

  • CVE Detection — Matches detected technologies against NVD with verified & possible exploit mapping

  • Subdomain Enumeration — Discovers exposed subdomains across multiple intelligence sources
  • Continuous Monitoring — Automated email alerts when new vulnerabilities are detected
  • PDF Reports — Downloadable vulnerability assessment reports per scanned domain
  • Risk Scoring — Calculates security posture from headers, CVEs, tech stack, and surface area
